Leyla Bauer

My name is Hillary Leyla Bauer, though I've frequently gone by the name Leyla I. Royale throughout my life. I have an extensive background in lots of fascinating jobs. I've worked as a professional rock cellist, I've been a flying trapeze instructor, and one of my favorite jobs was working as a haunted house clown.

I'm learning front and back end development to further my career and to help out my peers in one of my favorite industires I've worked in --tourism and history. I've been a professional tour guide in Chicago since 2011 and have researched, written, and led dozens of tours all over the city. While I love research, there are some limitations to it, and my goal is to be able to make accessing historical records easier and to create interfaces with which the general public can both find and record their own histories.

The first way I'm looking to do this is by creating a database of the address conversions that happened in Chicago in 1909. That year, the entire city went from its previous addresses to the order we're familliar with today. If you want to figure out the old address of a building, you have to hunt through a PDF scan of a book from 1909 listing every street and its new/old address. My first project as a developer is to create a website in which researchers like myself can simply enter in the new (or old) address to find its partner without having to scan through an old PDF.

It's a simple idea that requires a lot of data entry, but with fellow historians to help enter addresses, it'll be a site that ultimately saves time for researchers and allows easier access for casual historians to look up the history of a specific building. With skills I'll learn in Python and basic front end development, I'm excited to bring the site to life by the end of the summer.

I'm Leyla!
